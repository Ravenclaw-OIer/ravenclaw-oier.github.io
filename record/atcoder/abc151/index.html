AtCoder Beginner Contest 151 Record

Posted on 2020-01-20

This contest was virtually participated. Contest rating was not changed.

Problem D. Maze Master

This is the simplest problem D I have seen in ABC series. Use BFS to calculate the distance between $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ and store them somewhere. The overall complexity is $O(H^2W^2)$ with a very small constant.

This problem can also be solved with Floyd-Warshall, but I personally think it’s too complicated.

Problem E. Max-Min Sums

This is also pretty simple. The key observation is that we should count all numbers’ contribution to maximum numbers and minimum numbers, which means we should calculate the occurrences of a number’s being the largest element in its subset.

For a number located at position $i$, it can only be the largest element if $i>k$, in this case it has $\text{C}^{k-1}_{i-1}$ times of being chosen as the largest element. Similarly, it can only be the smallest element if $i+k-1\le n$, in this case it has $\text{C}^{k-1}_{n-i}$ times of being the smallest element.

We can then calculate the sum. The complexity is $O(n \log n)$.

Problem F. Enclose All

If it was not for the silly mistake of wrong parameter order, I would’ve solved it in contest…

This is an easy geometry problem, just choose three points that are not on a line, and calculate the smallest circle that fits them in, and calculate how big the circle should be to enclose all points, or choose two points and do the same thing, among all the radii got, choose the minimum one.

The complexity is $O(n^4)$.
